Adaptable Phantom Prototype

Abstract

This research project focused on the development of an adaptable phantom prototype, designed for use in medical imaging and radiotherapy. The study utilized advanced CAD modeling techniques for component design and leveraged 3D printing technology for prototype fabrication.

Methodology

The research methodology encompassed the following key steps:

  • Conceptual design and requirements analysis
  • CAD modeling of individual components using SolidWorks
  • 3D printing of prototype parts using PLA material
  • Assembly and integration of electronic components
  • Calibration and testing of adaptability features

Results and Discussion

The project yielded a functional adaptable phantom prototype with the following key features:

  • Adjustable anatomical features for various imaging scenarios
  • Ergonomic design for ease of use in clinical settings
  • Interchangeable components for different imaging modalities
  • Integrated sensors for real-time feedback

Initial testing demonstrated improved adaptability and accuracy in medical imaging simulations compared to conventional phantoms.

Conclusion

The development of this adaptable phantom prototype represents a significant advancement in the field of medical imaging and radiotherapy. Its adaptability and precision offer new possibilities for training and research, potentially improving patient outcomes.
