Roller Coaster Simulation

Abstract

This project aims to simulate the motion of a rollercoaster-cart over a track. The cart is subject to gravity, friction, and air resistance. The cart is subjected to an initial thrust by a PMLSM motor which provides thrust for 4 seconds over a horizontal track. The goal is to analyze the forces acting on the cart and to simulate the motion of the cart over the track. The track is composed of different sections with varying slopes (horizontal and inclined). The simulation results will be used to analyze the forces acting on the cart and determine whether the thrust provided by the motor is sufficient to move the cart over the track.

Methodology

The project methodology included the following steps:

  • Mathematical modeling of roller coaster dynamics
  • Development of simulation software using Matlab/Simulink
  • Implementation of two interconnected subsystems: PMLSM motor and roller coaster cart
  • Incorporation of environmental factors such as gravity, friction, and air resistance
  • Validation of simulation results using conservation of energy principle

Results and Discussion

The simulation provided valuable insights into the following aspects:

  • Thrust provided by the PMLSM motor over time
  • Velocity and position of the cart over the track
  • Forces acting on the cart (gravitational force, frictional force, air resistance)
  • Analysis of whether the thrust provided by the motor is sufficient to move the cart over the track

The results demonstrated the importance of precise calculations and simulations in the design of roller coasters, allowing for the optimization of various parameters such as track profile, motor thrust, and environmental factors.

Conclusion

The simulation of the rollercoaster-cart over a track has been successfully implemented using Matlab/Simulink. The simulation results have been used to analyze the forces acting on the cart and to determine whether the thrust provided by the motor is sufficient to move the cart over the track. The results obtained from the simulation can be used as a reference for the development of roller coaster systems in the real world. With the development of computing power, we can simulate more complex systems and analyze the performance of the system before actual implementation.
