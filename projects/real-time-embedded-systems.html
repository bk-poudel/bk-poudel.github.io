Handheld Bio-Printer Prototype

Abstract

This research project focused on the development of a handheld bio-printer prototype, designed for precise bio-ink application in tissue engineering applications. The study utilized advanced CAD modeling techniques for component design and leveraged 3D printing technology for prototype fabrication.

Methodology

The research methodology encompassed the following key steps:

  • Conceptual design and requirements analysis
  • CAD modeling of individual components using SolidWorks
  • 3D printing of prototype parts using PLA material
  • Assembly and integration of electronic components
  • Calibration and testing of bio-ink dispensing mechanism

Results and Discussion

The project yielded a functional handheld bio-printer prototype with the following key features:

  • Precision bio-ink dispensing with adjustable flow rates
  • Ergonomic design for ease of use in laboratory settings
  • Interchangeable nozzle system for various bio-ink viscosities
  • Integrated temperature control for bio-ink preservation

Initial testing demonstrated improved precision in bio-ink deposition compared to conventional methods, with a positioning accuracy of ±50 μm.

Conclusion

The development of this handheld bio-printer prototype represents a significant advancement in the field of tissue engineering. Its precision and versatility offer new possibilities for in situ bioprinting applications, potentially accelerating research in regenerative medicine and personalized tissue engineering.
