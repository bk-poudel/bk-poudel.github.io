3 DOF Robotic Arm with Soft Robotics Manipulator

Project Overview

This project was developed at the Flexible AI Enabled Mechatronics Systems (FAMS) Lab, NYU Tandon School of Engineering, as part of a multidisciplinary team. We designed and built a 3 Degree-of-Freedom robotic arm equipped with a soft robotics manipulator featuring three flexible fingers. The system demonstrates integration of mechanical design, electronics, and software for advanced manipulation tasks.

Figure: 3 DOF Robotic Arm with Soft 3-Finger Manipulator (FAMS Lab, NYU Tandon)

Team & My Role

  • Collaborated with a team of talented engineers and researchers
  • Responsible for mechanical structure design of the robotic arm
  • Developed motor and sensor control code for precise actuation
  • Integrated hardware and software for seamless operation

Key Features

Mechanical Design

Custom-designed 3 DOF arm structure for workspace flexibility and stability

Soft Manipulator

Three soft fingers enable adaptive grasping and safe interaction with delicate objects

Motor & Sensor Integration

Motor drivers and sensor feedback for closed-loop control and precise positioning

Embedded Programming

Developed firmware for coordinated movement and sensor data acquisition

Conclusion

The project highlights the synergy of mechanical engineering, electronics, and software in robotics. The developed arm serves as a platform for research in soft manipulation and AI-enabled mechatronics, with potential applications in adaptive grasping and safe human-robot interaction.
